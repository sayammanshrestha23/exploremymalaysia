Essential Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before visiting Malaysia

🌎

Visa Information

Citizens of over 160 countries can enter Malaysia visa-free for up to 90 days. Check the official Immigration Department website for the latest requirements.

☔

Best Time to Visit

Malaysia is tropical year-round. The west coast (Langkawi, Penang) is best from November–April. East coast destinations are best from March–October.

💲

Currency & Budget

The Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) is the currency. Malaysia is budget-friendly — street food costs RM3–8, and quality hotels start from RM80–150 per night.

🚌

Getting Around

Kuala Lumpur has an excellent LRT/MRT network. Grab (ride-hailing) is widely used. Intercity travel is convenient by bus, train (KTM), or budget airline.

📱

Connectivity

Malaysia has excellent 4G/5G coverage in urban areas. Purchase a local SIM card at the airport (RM30–50 for 10–30GB data) for affordable connectivity.

🏞

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly when visiting temples and mosques. Remove shoes before entering homes. Accept food and drinks with your right hand as a sign of respect.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers for travellers planning their Malaysia trip

Malaysia is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. The country has a low violent crime rate compared to many destinations. As with any travel, exercise normal precautions — keep valuables secure, stay aware in crowded areas, and use registered taxis or Grab. The tourist police are helpful in major cities.
Bahasa Malaysia (Malay) is the official language, but English is widely spoken throughout the country, especially in cities, hotels, and tourist areas. You will have no difficulty communicating in English as a visitor.

No specific vaccinations are legally required for entry to Malaysia (unless coming from a yellow fever endemic country). However, it is recommended to be up to date on routine vaccinations. Consult your doctor 4–6 weeks before travel for personalised advice.
Tipping is not traditionally expected in Malaysia — service charges (typically 10%) are usually included in restaurant bills. That said, tips are appreciated for excellent service at upscale restaurants, by tour guides, and by hotel staff. Rounding up taxi fares is also common courtesy.
